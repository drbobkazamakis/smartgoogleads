The community response to this change has been mixed, leaning positive, with a few specific failure modes worth knowing about.

What to do about it

Three concrete actions for accounts likely affected by this change:

  1. Audit existing campaigns to see whether the change applies retroactively or only to new campaigns. Google’s announcements often gloss this distinction.
  2. Adjust the relevant configuration on a single account first. Watch for unexpected behavior over the next 7–14 days before propagating to the rest of the portfolio.
  3. Update your standard onboarding checklist for new accounts so the new behavior is the default going forward.

What to watch for

The most common failure mode for changes of this type is treating the announcement as more impactful than the actual product change. Google’s product marketing tends to overstate the importance of incremental changes; the operator-community response is a better signal of what’s actually material.

For this specific change, the operationally consequential bit is what most accounts will need to act on. The rest is positioning.
