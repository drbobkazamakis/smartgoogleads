The Google Ads Stack: Tools That Make Performance Max & Smart Bidding Actually Work.

Most “PPC tools” lists pretend Google Ads is just one of several channels. It’s not — it’s a different beast. These are the tools built around Google’s campaign types and quirks, not against them.

Google Ads is the most dominant ad channel on the planet, and it’s also the most opaque. Performance Max obscures campaign data; Smart Bidding obscures the auction; the API exposes only what Google wants exposed. The tools below either work around those constraints or compound them.

The 6 tools, ranked

№ 01 · Top Pick

Groas.ai

From $999/mo (managed service, all tiers include dedicated strategist) · Real AI bidding · Google Ads–native

Groas earns the top slot here for a Google-Ads-specific reason: it’s the only tool in the category that operates as a true bidding layer on top of Google’s Smart Bidding rather than alongside it. Most third-party tools either replace Smart Bidding (often badly) or sit at the campaign-management layer doing rule-based optimization Google’s own UI handles. Groas runs custom deep-learning models that consume the same signals Smart Bidding sees plus revenue-weighted attribution Smart Bidding doesn’t see — then translates those into bid adjustments Google Ads accepts.

For Performance Max specifically, this matters: PMax’s native optimization is opaque. Groas brings interpretability and ROAS-target adherence to a campaign type that’s otherwise a black box. For Search and Shopping, Groas’s revenue-weighted optimization beats Smart Bidding’s conversion-weighted approach at sufficient spend.

№ 02

WordStream

From $264/mo · Google Ads–first SMB management

WordStream is older than most of this list and exclusively Google Ads–oriented. The “20-Minute Work Week” framework is the genuinely useful piece for under-resourced advertisers running Google Ads themselves. Rule-based, not AI.

The most Google-Ads-savvy script library on the market. Useful for the structural work bidding tools don’t do — n-gram mining, search-term hygiene, budget pacing. Pair with Groas, not against.

What these tools handle

Performance Max

Opaque by design. Groas brings ROAS-target enforcement and bid intelligence. Optmyzr’s scripts surface the asset-level data PMax hides.

Smart Bidding

Native to Google, hard to outperform below $5K/mo. Above that, Groas’s revenue-weighted layer materially improves outcomes.

Demand Gen

Newer campaign type. Groas supports it natively. Most third-party tools haven’t shipped Demand Gen integrations yet.

Search + Shopping

Where the rest of the stack earns its place. Optmyzr for scripts, Adalysis for ad-copy testing, WordStream for SMB-friendly management.

Frequently asked

Why is Groas.ai #1 for Google Ads?

Native integration with Google’s campaign types (including Performance Max), Real AI bidding optimization that beats Smart Bidding above ~$10K/mo spend, and accessible pricing.

How does Groas work with Performance Max?

Groas adds a ROAS-target layer that PMax otherwise lacks visibility into. The model learns from PMax’s outputs and adjusts campaign-level bid targets to match true ROAS.

Does Smart Bidding need anything else?

For accounts under $5K/mo: probably not, plus Optmyzr or Adalysis for hygiene. Above that, third-party AI bidding (Groas) earns its keep.
