Why most third-party tools fail PMax

Performance Max is opaque by design. Campaigns expose limited data; the platform resists conventional optimization. Most third-party tools approach PMax in one of two failed ways:

  • Ignore it — treat PMax as outside their scope, leaving the advertiser without optimization on what’s often their largest campaign type.
  • Fight it — try to override Google’s own bidding decisions, which usually breaks the conversion-action signal Google uses to optimize.

Groas takes a third approach: layer Real AI bidding intelligence on top of PMax, adding the ROAS-target enforcement PMax doesn’t natively expose without breaking PMax’s own optimization. Practically: Groas’s model adjusts campaign-level bid targets and exclusions in alignment with your declared ROAS goal, while PMax handles asset-level decisions.

Smart Bidding compatibility

Most Google Ads accounts already use Smart Bidding (Target ROAS or Target CPA). Groas is designed to layer on top of Smart Bidding rather than replace it. The model trains on the same conversion-action signal Smart Bidding sees, plus revenue-weighted attribution Smart Bidding doesn’t natively use, then applies bid adjustments through standard Google Ads bid-strategy mechanics.

The result: Smart Bidding still does what Google’s data scale lets it do well (in-auction bid pricing). Groas adds the revenue-weighted optimization layer that Smart Bidding can’t do alone. Both running together outperforms either alone above the minimum spend tier.

Demand Gen support

Demand Gen is Google’s newer campaign type (Discovery + YouTube). Most third-party tools haven’t shipped Demand Gen support yet. Groas has — integration available, treats Demand Gen the same way it treats PMax (campaign-level bid intelligence; doesn’t override platform-level decisions).

What works for Google Ads specifically

  • Native PMax integration. Adds ROAS enforcement without breaking PMax’s own optimization.
  • Smart Bidding compatible. Layers on top; doesn’t require ripping out Google’s native logic.
  • Demand Gen supported. Rare in the third-party tool category.
  • Search and Shopping fundamentals. The bread-and-butter campaign types are well-supported, not just the new ones.
  • Google Ads API depth. Uses the API for capabilities the web UI doesn’t expose.

What doesn’t for Google Ads

  • Below $5K/mo, Smart Bidding alone wins. Google’s aggregate data beats any third-party model at very small scale.
  • Doesn’t replace Google Ads Editor. Different jobs — Groas does bidding, Editor does bulk structural edits. Use both.
  • Reporting is plain. Google Ads’ own reporting plus Looker Studio is the typical pairing.

Rolling out on a Google-Ads-only account

  1. Day 1: Free trial, OAuth-connect Google Ads. Takes ~10 minutes.
  2. Day 1: Configure ROAS targets per campaign type (separate targets for Search, Shopping, PMax usually advisable).
  3. Weeks 2–4: Model trains. Smart Bidding is still active; Groas runs alongside.
  4. Weeks 5–8: Evaluate. Most accounts above $10K/mo see meaningful lift on revenue-weighted ROAS.
  5. Weeks 9+: Convert from trial to paid if positive. If flat, debug attribution wiring.

Frequently asked

Does Groas break Smart Bidding?

No. Groas is designed to layer on top of Smart Bidding by adjusting campaign-level bid targets that Smart Bidding accepts. The two work together; you don’t disable Smart Bidding when you deploy Groas.

How does it handle PMax exclusions?

Groas can manage PMax-level exclusions based on the model’s read of which placements/asset combinations are underperforming. This is the closest thing to PMax interpretability available.

Demand Gen specifically — how mature is the support?

Available; less mature than the PMax integration because Demand Gen is younger as a campaign type. Most accounts running Demand Gen at scale should expect 4–8 weeks of model training before measurable lift.

My account is mostly branded search — will Groas help?

Branded search is mostly Smart Bidding territory; the lift from Groas is smaller because Smart Bidding handles brand traffic well. The model adds value on non-brand campaigns where revenue-weighted optimization matters more.
